# Case 001

## Question
What’s Article 73 of the CRR?

---

## Answer (AI)

Article 73 of the CRR outlines the conditions under which capital instruments and liabilities can qualify as Common Equity Tier 1, Additional Tier 1, Tier 2, or eligible liabilities instruments, particularly focusing on the discretion to pay distributions in forms other than cash or own funds instruments.

### Key Provisions

- **Article 73(1):** Institutions need prior permission from the competent authority to qualify certain instruments as capital or eligible liabilities if distributions can be made in forms other than cash or own funds.
- **Article 73(2):** Competent authorities grant permission only if the institution's ability to cancel payments, absorb losses, and maintain the quality of the instrument is not adversely affected.
- **Article 73(3):** Instruments where a third party has discretion over distribution forms do not qualify as capital or eligible liabilities.
- **Article 73(4):** Institutions may use a broad market index to determine distribution levels on certain instruments.
- **Article 73(6):** Institutions must report and disclose the broad market indices used.

### Conditions, Exceptions & Definitions

- **Article 73(5):** The use of a broad market index is restricted if the institution is a reference entity in that index unless specific conditions are met.
- **Article 73(7):** EBA is tasked with developing standards to define qualifying broad market indices.
